## Overview

Standard distributed data parallel (DDP) spends most of its time moving gradients across the network, which becomes the bottleneck the moment you leave a single-node setup. Hermes attacks that at the kernel level.

The system implements gradient compression in custom CUDA kernels running at GPU granularity, cutting compression latency from 160ms to 3.5ms (about 46x) and shrinking communication bandwidth by up to 13,000x compared to DDP.

On top of that, it introduces heterogeneity-aware sharding — dividing work across mixed hardware so LLM training runs across a fleet of non-matching GPUs connected over commodity WiFi, not just a homogeneous cluster.
